How it works

TRANSFER STATUSES DOCUMENTS IBAN SEPA

Transfer statuses:


  • Requested - Invoice for Transfer was requested in a system.

  • Sent - Payment proof for Transfer was uploaded to the system and checked by AnyWires accountant.

  • Received - Transfer have been received to the bank.

  • Approved - merchant uploaded all the documents (Proof + client's KYC) to transfer and it was reviewed and Approved by AnyWires compliance.

  • Available - after settlement delay period Transfer become available for settlement (now merchant can see this transfer when requesting Settlement

  • Settled- Transfer was settled to Merchant by settlement request.

  • Declined - Transfer was declined by any reason.

  • Frozen - Set upon freezing any of bank accounts, setting manually based on the frozen amount.

  • Recall - Set upon getting notice from the bank that client issued a recall.


Wire Transfers workflow:

Documents uploading and Verification

All the documents connected to Wire Transfer should be uploaded in Invoice popUp in Invoice List page.

Proof of Payment

To find wire transfer in a bank system AnyWires accountant need to have ones payment proof (to see clients legal name, senders details and so on).

So to make WireTransfer Received Merchant need to upload Proof of Payment which contains:

  • • Payer's name

  • • Payer's Bank's name and Beneficiary IBAN

  • • Date payment was (will be) made

  • • Amount and currency of payment.

KYC of a Client:

To make Transfer Approved after it was received AnyWires compliance need to check payer's KYC. Merchant need to upload client's ID and Utility Bill, and after it would be reviewed and approved - Transfer would be marked as Approved and get a V sign.

  • • ID should be clear contains full client's details.

  • • Utility Bill should contain payer's address and name, and to be not older than 3 month.

SEPA

Please mind that you can make SEPA wires only between SEPA countries.

You cannot make SEPA wire from Africa or Wakanda, because they are not in the SEPA zone.

There are 28 EU member states and a number of additional SEPA zone territories.

The 28 EU States:

Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovak Republic, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, and United Kingdom.
